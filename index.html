Nature and Scope of Agreement: Bed Bug Inspection and Treatment Plan.
The agreement contained within is for the management of bed bugs. In low and moderate level infestations with proper client cooperation elimination is possible, however, in high level infestations, there is no guarantee that bed bugs will be eliminated or prevented from continuing to infest the treated areas. Bed bugs are an introduced pest and the re-occurrence of bed bugs within the facility can occur at any given time.

The 5 Steps to Eliminating Bed Bugs
Step 1     Verification & Estimate
Step 2     Initial Intensive Treatment  This treatment will be performed once bed buys are verified to be present in the structure.
Step 3     Follow-up Services
The following contact information is being provided to Interstate Pest Management so follow-ups can be scheduled and confirmed.
  • Follow-up services will be conducted during which time the structure will be inspected and treated as necessary based upon inspection findings.
  • Follow-up visits will be scheduled at 10-12 days following the initial treatment and will continue at approximately fourteen day intervals until either 1) Control is declared. 2) Client notifies the company that they want the program to end; thereby sacrificing control (Step 5) and elimination (Step 6). If this action is taken no service plan will be granted. In high level infestations follow-ups may occur more frequently.
  • The company will contact the contracting party 3-7 days prior to the scheduled follow-up to remind and confirm the appointment. The company will consider the appointment confirmed unless we hear directly from the contracting party changing the scheduled appointment. The client agrees that if the appointment is confirmed and we arrive and cannot gain access to the structure that the client is responsible for 40% of the follow-up treatment and that the treatment must be rescheduled for the full follow-up fee.
Step 4     Elimination

During the follow-up visits (but not before the second visit) an evaluation will be made to determine if control has been achieved. The company will declare ELIMINATION when no more bed bugs are observed during the follow-up visit AND the client has not reported any visible bed bug activity or experienced any bites during the immediate preceding 14 day period. If bites are occurring or bed bugs are found to be present then follow-up visits will continue (at the above follow-up fee) until ELIMINATION can be declared or client wishes to end the agreement.

Step 5     Warranty

The client has a 45 day warranty after ELIMINATION has been declared. It is understood that in some situations, the infestation may never be declared “eliminated”. In these cases ongoing services will be required to manage the bed bug infestation and no warranty will be granted. If during the elimination and warranty period additional furniture (new or used) is brought into the structure the warranty period may be void.
